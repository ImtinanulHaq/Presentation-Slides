── PATTERN 1.1: EXECUTIVE BUSINESS PRESENTATION ──
Target Audience: C-Suite, Board Members, Investors
Tone: Professional, Data-Driven, Concise
Typical Duration: 15-20 minutes (10-12 slides)
Decision Threshold: Executive-level buying decision or strategy approval

REQUIRED ELEMENTS:
1. Executive Summary Slide
   - Clear value proposition (one sentence)
   - Three key metrics supporting the proposal
   - Expected timeline and ROI

2. Problem/Opportunity Analysis
   - Quantified market gap or business challenge
   - Financial impact of current state
   - Competitive pressure or market trend

3. Proposed Solution
   - Specific approach with differentiation
   - Implementation timeline (phased)
   - Risk mitigation strategies

4. Financial Impact
   - Cost-Benefit Analysis table
   - ROI projection (1, 3, 5-year)
   - Payback period

5. Implementation Roadmap
   - Critical milestones (quarters or phases)
   - Resource requirements
   - Success metrics and KPIs

6. Team & Execution Capability
   - Key stakeholders and responsibilities
   - Track record in similar initiatives
   - Organizational readiness assessment

STRUCTURE RULES:
- Maximum 4 bullet points per slide (executive preference)
- Each bullet ≤ 15 words
- Use numbers/percentages liberally
- Avoid jargon unless context-appropriate
- One key message per slide

VISUAL ELEMENTS:
- Icons: chart.ascending, briefcase, shield.checkmark, target
- Colors: Professional (Blue #003366, Gray #666666, White background)
- Charts: Bar charts for comparisons, Line graphs for trends
- Layouts: Title + 3 content area (top: metrics, middle: main message, bottom: CTA)

── PATTERN 1.2: SALES PITCH DECK ──
Target Audience: Prospects, Sales Teams, Channel Partners
Tone: Persuasive, Customer-Focused, Problem-Solution
Typical Duration: 20-30 minutes (8-10 slides)
Decision Threshold: Customer acquisition or partnership agreement

ARCHITECTURE:
1. Hook Slide (Problem)
   - Customer's pain point (stated as question)
   - Business impact (time/cost/quality)
   - Audience relevance ("This affects you if...")

2. Current State Analysis
   - Customer's current approach
   - Limitations and costs
   - Industry benchmarks (showing gap)

3. Competitive Landscape
   - Market overview
   - Alternative approaches (and their limitations)
   - Why competing solutions fall short

4. Proposed Solution
   - Product/service overview
   - How it addresses the specific pain point
   - Key differentiators (3-5 maximum)

5. Use Case/Social Proof
   - Real customer success story
   - Specific metrics achieved
   - Quote from customer (authentic)

6. Implementation & Support
   - How we deliver the solution
   - Timeline to value
   - Support structure and SLAs

7. Pricing & Investment
   - Transparent pricing model
   - Payment terms and options
   - ROI justification

8. Call to Action
   - Next steps (specific and time-bound)
   - Contact information
   - Special offer or limited-time incentive

BULLET OPTIMIZATION:
Each bullet follows formula: [Benefit] + [Evidence/How] = Compelling Statement
Examples:
✓ "Cut implementation time from 16 weeks to 4 weeks through automated setup"
✓ "Reduce support tickets by 70% via intelligent triage and self-service"
✓ "Improve team adoption from 40% to 95% with intuitive onboarding"

VISUAL STRATEGY:
- Icons: target, rocket.launch, handshake, chart, user.check
- Colors: Brand colors for consistency, contrasting highlight color
- Images: Customer environments, success metrics, product in action
- Avoid: Cluttered layouts, generic stock photos

── PATTERN 1.3: EDUCATIONAL/TRAINING PRESENTATION ──
Target Audience: Students, Employees, Learners
Tone: Clear, Structured, Supportive
Typical Duration: 45-90 minutes (15-25 slides depending on depth)
Success Metric: Comprehension and skill acquisition

STRUCTURE:
1. Learning Objectives Slide
   - 3-5 clear, measurable objectives
   - Expected outcomes ("By end of session, you will...")
   - Success criteria

2. Content Progression (Conceptual Build)
   - Start with familiar concepts
   - Progress to complex topics
   - Layer complexity gradually
   - Use analogies for abstract concepts

3. Key Concept Slides (One per major concept)
   - Definition (simple language)
   - Visual representation (diagram/icon)
   - Real-world example
   - Common misconception addressed

4. Application/Practice Slides
   - How to apply the concept
   - Step-by-step examples
   - Interactive element (questions for reflection)

5. Summary/Review
   - Key takeaways (reiterate 3-5 main points)
   - Connection to broader context
   - Transition to next topic

6. Resources & Further Learning
   - Recommended readings
   - Practice exercises
   - Contact info for questions

PEDAGOGICAL RULES:
- Use consistent terminology throughout
- Define all specialized terms on first use
- Provide visual representations of abstract concepts
- Include examples that span difficulty levels
- Encourage note-taking with white space
- Avoid reading slides verbatim (slides support, you deliver)

VISUAL APPROACH:
- Icons: book, lightbulb, check, question, arrow.progression
- Colors: Calm, professional (avoid overstimulation)
- Diagrams: Process flows, concept maps, timelines
- Typography: Large, readable fonts

SECTION 2: CONTENT TRANSFORMATION RULES
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RULE 2.1: WORD COUNT TO SLIDE COUNT MAPPING
Raw Content Words → Recommended Slide Count

100-200 words      → 3-4 slides (summary presentation)
200-400 words      → 4-5 slides (brief overview)
400-700 words      → 6-8 slides (standard presentation)
700-1200 words     → 8-12 slides (comprehensive)
1200-2000 words    → 12-16 slides (in-depth)
2000+ words        → 16-20+ slides (workshop/course)

RULE 2.2: CONTENT ANALYSIS & RESTRUCTURING

If Input Contains:
→ Dense Paragraphs: Break into 3-4 logical slides
→ Multiple Disconnected Topics: Add section divider slides
→ No Statistics/Metrics: Transform qualitative claims to quantitative
→ Weak Introduction: Create compelling hook slide
→ Vague Conclusions: Add clear call-to-action slide
→ Jargon Heavy: Simplify terminology for audience
→ Lists Without Context: Group into thematic bullets
→ Story/Case Study: Structure as problem-solution-result progression

RULE 2.3: BULLET POINT TRANSFORMATION

WEAK INPUT:
"Our company provides solutions for enterprise customers that help them manage
their business processes more effectively through the use of advanced technology
and automation capabilities that reduce manual work and improve efficiency metrics
across the organization."

TRANSFORMED OUTPUT:
✓ "Automate 70% of manual processes in first 90 days"
✓ "Improve team productivity by average 3.2 hours per person per week"
✓ "Achieve ROI within 6 months with measurable cost reduction"

RULE 2.4: TONE ADJUSTMENT

Input Tone: Professional/Corporate
→ Use: Formal language, data-driven examples, business focus
→ Bullet Structure: [Benefit] + [Business Impact] = Result
→ Visual Tone: Minimalist icons, professional colors (Blue, Gray, Black)
→ Example Bullet: "Reduce operational costs by $500K annually through automation"

Input Tone: Casual/Friendly
→ Use: Conversational language, relatable examples, approachable tone
→ Bullet Structure: [Relatable Benefit] + [How] = Easy Understanding
→ Visual Tone: Colorful icons, friendly symbols, vibrant palette
→ Example Bullet: "Save your team 5 hours every week (that's 260 hours/year!)"

Input Tone: Academic/Technical
→ Use: Precise terminology, sources/citations, detailed explanations
→ Bullet Structure: [Concept] + [Explanation] + [Implication] = Learning
→ Visual Tone: Scientific icons, educational diagrams, neutral colors
→ Example Bullet: "Reduces latency by 45% through optimized query compilation"

Input Tone: Persuasive/Sales
→ Use: Question-based openings, problem emphasis, solution highlight
→ Bullet Structure: [Problem] + [Solution] + [Benefit] = Conversion
→ Visual Tone: Bold icons, impactful symbols, high-contrast colors
→ Example Bullet: "Stop losing customers to faster competitors—match speed in weeks"

SECTION 4: LAYOUT & DESIGN PRINCIPLES
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STANDARD SLIDE LAYOUT TYPES:

Type A: Title Slide
- Large, bold title (60pt+)
- Subtitle supporting main message (36pt)
- Optional: Background image, brand element

Type B: Title + Content (4 Bullets)
- Title: 48pt, bold
- 4 bullet points: 24pt, single line each (max 15 words)
- Left 70% content, right 30% visual/icon

Type C: Title + Two Column
- Title: 44pt
- Left column: 3-4 bullets (24pt)
- Right column: Visual, chart, or image
- Optimal for comparisons or process flows

Type D: Title + Large Visual
- Title: 44pt
- Content area: Large diagram, chart, or infographic
- Minimal text (6-8 words supporting visual)

Type E: Section Divider
- Large, bold topic (64pt)
- Background color or image
- Minimal text, high visual impact

DESIGN RULES:
1. Consistency: Same font family, color palette, layout throughout
2. Whitespace: Minimum 15% of slide is empty space
3. Contrast: Readable text on background (21:1 text:background minimum)
4. Visual Hierarchy: Title > Body > Supporting details
5. One Idea Per Slide: Avoid cramming multiple concepts
6. Alignment: Text/graphics aligned to grids (clean appearance)

PATTERN 5.1: PROBLEM-SOLUTION-RESULT (PSR) NARRATIVE
Used For: Sales, Pitches, Case Studies

Structure:
Slide 1: Define the PROBLEM
- Specific customer pain point
- Why it matters (business impact)
- Emotional resonance ("If this happens to you...")

Slide 2: Show Current Struggles
- Status quo costs (time, money, quality)
- Existing solution limitations
- Competitive disadvantages

Slide 3: Introduce SOLUTION
- Your offering (specific, not vague)
- Key differentiators (3 max)
- Why it's better than alternatives

Slide 4: RESULTS/PROOF
- Customer testimonial + metrics
- Before/after comparison
- Timeline to impact

Example Transformation:
Input: "We have a project management tool."
Output:
- S1: "60% of projects miss deadlines—why? Disconnected team communication."
- S2: "Current tools lack real-time collaboration. Teams lose 12+ hours/week switching between apps."
- S3: "Unified platform: one dashboard, real-time updates, automated status reporting."
- R1: "Acme Corp reduced missed deadlines by 85% in first quarter."

PATTERN 5.2: DATA-DRIVEN PRESENTATION
Used For: Executive briefings, Research presentations, Analytics reports

Structure:
1. Data Context Slide
- What data you're presenting
- Time period and sources
- Key limitations to acknowledge

2. Primary Finding Slide
- Headline with key metric
- Supporting data visualization
- One insight per slide

3. Secondary Insight Slide
- Second most important finding
- Chart/graph with clear labeling
- Business implication bullet

4. Deeper Analysis (if warranted)
- Trend analysis
- Segment breakdown
- Correlation insights

5. Recommendations/Actions
- Data-backed recommendations
- Prioritized by impact
- Clear next steps

Visual Rules for Data:
- Use same chart type for comparisons
- Label axes clearly
- Highlight key data point (color emphasis)
- Avoid 3D charts (distort perception)
- Legends should be minimal

SECTION 6: ANTI-PATTERNS TO AVOID
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: WALL OF TEXT
Problem: Bullet points with 20+ words, multiple sentences per bullet
Fix: Max 12-15 words per bullet, one idea per bullet

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: CHART OVERLOAD
Problem: Too many data points, 3D charts, unprofessional formatting
Fix: One insight per chart, clear labeling, professional styling

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: INCONSISTENT DESIGN
Problem: Different fonts, colors, layouts across slides
Fix: Use design system, consistent templates, brand guidelines

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: UNCLEAR OBJECTIVES
Problem: Audience doesn't know why they're watching
Fix: Open with clear objective, reiterate as needed, close with takeaway

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: OUTDATED DATA
Problem: Statistics from 3+ years ago presented as current
Fix: Include data date, use recent sources, acknowledge historical data

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 6: IRRELEVANT IMAGES
Problem: Stock photos that don't reinforce message
Fix: Choose images that directly support content, consider removing if neutral

❌ ANTI-PATTERN 7: ABSENCE OF SPEAKER NOTES
Problem: Slides work but presenter has no guidance
Fix: Include context, key talking points, transitions in notes
